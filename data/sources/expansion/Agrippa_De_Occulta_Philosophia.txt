DE OCCULTA PHILOSOPHIA
By Heinrich Cornelius Agrippa (1486-1535)

BOOK III: DEMONOLGY AND THE ART OF MAGIC

Chapter 14: Of the Spirits and Demons

The first book treats of natural philosophy, the second of celestial influences; this third book concerns the spirits and demons. For as the stars are divided into seven planets, so are there seven orders of angels and demons.

The first order is called Seraphim, burning with love to God.
The second Cherubim, full of wisdom.
The third Thrones, bearing the divine will.
The fourth Dominions, ruling over lesser spirits.
The fifth Virtues, executing celestial functions.
The sixth Powers, commanding legions.
The seventh Principalities, governing regions.

Below these are the Archangels, Angels, and finally the demons proper.

The demons are divided into seven spheres:
1. The sphere of Saturn - cold and dry spirits
2. The sphere of Jupiter - windy and moist spirits
3. The sphere of Mars - fiery and hot spirits
4. The sphere of the Sun - luminous and active spirits
5. The sphere of Venus - amorous and gentle spirits
6. The sphere of Mercury - swift and cunning spirits
7. The sphere of the Moon - watery and changeable spirits

Each demon has its own name, nature, and power. They can be summoned through proper rituals, invocations, and correspondences.

The art of magic consists in:
1. Understanding the nature of spirits
2. Learning their names and hierarchies
3. Establishing proper correspondences (planetary, elemental, temporal)
4. Performing rituals with correct materials and timing
5. Maintaining purity of intent and body

Chapter 15: The Seven Spirits of Mercury

Mercury governs Wednesday, the element of air, and the planet Mercury itself.
The spirits under Mercury's dominion are:

1. Vapula - King of the first sphere of Mercury
   - Nature: Swift, cunning, messenger-like
   - Correspondence: Wednesday, Air, Mercury
   - Material: Quick-silver, glass, mirrors
   - Invocation: "Vapula, king of spirits swift and keen,
     By Mercury's power I thee invoke,
     Come to my aid, grant me thy boon,
     In name of Father, Son, and Holy Ghost."

2. Flegias - Second spirit of Mercury
   - Nature: Fiery, passionate, quick-tempered
   - Correspondence: Wednesday, Fire, Mars aspect
   - Material: Iron, sulfur, red cloth
   - Invocation: "Flegias, spirit of fiery wrath,
     By Mercury's power I thee invoke,
     Grant me thy aid in all my need,
     In name of Father, Son, and Holy Ghost."

3. Forculus - Third spirit of Mercury
   - Nature: Gatekeeper, threshold spirit
   - Correspondence: Wednesday, Air, doorways
   - Material: Brass, keys, iron bars
   - Invocation: "Forculus, keeper of gates and doors,
     By Mercury's power I thee invoke,
     Open thy passage to my request,
     In name of Father, Son, and Holy Ghost."

4. Megapenthes - Fourth spirit of Mercury
   - Nature: Joyful, celebratory, festive
   - Correspondence: Wednesday, Air, music
   - Material: Wine, instruments, flowers
   - Invocation: "Megapenthes, spirit of joy and mirth,
     By Mercury's power I thee invoke,
     Grant me gladness in all my works,
     In name of Father, Son, and Holy Ghost."

5. Pan - Fifth spirit of Mercury
   - Nature: Wild, rustic, nature-bound
   - Correspondence: Wednesday, Earth, forests
   - Material: Wood, herbs, animal skins
   - Invocation: "Pan, lord of wild things and woods,
     By Mercury's power I thee invoke,
     Grant me protection in all my travels,
     In name of Father, Son, and Holy Ghost."

6. Panthea - Sixth spirit of Mercury
   - Nature: Universal, encompassing, divine
   - Correspondence: Wednesday, Air, cosmos
   - Material: Crystal, spheres, celestial charts
   - Invocation: "Panthea, spirit of all things,
     By Mercury's power I thee invoke,
     Grant me wisdom to understand,
     In name of Father, Son, and Holy Ghost."

7. Panga - Seventh spirit of Mercury
   - Nature: Warrior, protective, fierce
   - Correspondence: Wednesday, Fire, defense
   - Material: Steel, armor, weapons
   - Invocation: "Panga, warrior spirit strong and true,
     By Mercury's power I thee invoke,
     Grant me protection from all harm,
     In name of Father, Son, and Holy Ghost."

These spirits can be summoned for various purposes:
- Vapula for messages and swift communication
- Flegias for passionate endeavors
- Forculus for opening doors and passages
- Megapenthes for joy and celebration
- Pan for protection in wild places
- Panthea for universal wisdom
- Panga for defense against enemies

WARNING: The summoning of demons requires:
1. Pure intent - never for evil purposes
2. Proper materials - clean, consecrated objects
3. Correct timing - appropriate planetary hours
4. Ritual purity - bathed, clothed in white
5. Invocation formula - always invoking the Trinity

The spirits respond to their proper names and can be commanded through:
- Direct address by full name
- Reference to their sphere of dominion
- Appeal to their nature and character
- Promise of reward (incense, wine, praise)

Remember: All magic must honor the divine order and never contravene natural law.
