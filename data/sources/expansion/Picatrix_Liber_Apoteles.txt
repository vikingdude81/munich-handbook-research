PICATRIX (THE BOOK OF SPOILS)
By Abu Bakr Muhammad ibn Ali al-Khwarizmi
Translated and adapted for the Western tradition

INTRODUCTION

Picatrix is the great Arabic work on magic, transmitted through Spain to Europe.
The name means "The Book of Spoils" or "The Book of Victory."

It treats of:
1. The nature of spirits and demons
2. Their correspondences with planets, elements, and times
3. Rituals for summoning and commanding them
4. Materials and tools required for each operation
5. The hierarchy of the spiritual world

BOOK I: THE SPIRITS AND THEIR NATURES

Chapter 1: The Seven Orders of Spirits

The spiritual world is divided into seven orders, corresponding to the seven planets:

1. Angels - Pure spirits, servants of God
2. Archangels - High spirits, commanders of legions
3. Demons - Lower spirits, subject to planetary influences
4. Genii - Protective spirits of places and objects
5. Elementals - Spirits of earth, water, air, fire
6. Undines - Water spirits
7. Sylphs - Air spirits

Each order has its own nature, powers, and limitations.

Chapter 2: The Planetary Hierarchy

The planets rule over specific types of spirits:

SATURN (Saturday)
- Spirits: Cold, dry, melancholic
- Nature: Wise, slow, contemplative
- Colors: Lead, black, white
- Metals: Iron, lead
- Stones: Lapis lazuli, hematite
- Herbs: Hellebore, mandrake

JUPITER (Thursday)
- Spirits: Windy, moist, expansive
- Nature: Generous, noble, philosophical
- Colors: Purple, blue, gold
- Metals: Tin, copper
- Stones: Sapphire, amethyst
- Herbs: Lavender, rosemary

MARS (Tuesday)
- Spirits: Fiery, hot, aggressive
- Nature: Warrior, protective, fierce
- Colors: Red, orange, black
- Metals: Iron, steel
- Stones: Ruby, garnet
- Herbs: Sage, thyme

SUN (Sunday)
- Spirits: Luminous, active, royal
- Nature: Majestic, commanding, brilliant
- Colors: Gold, yellow, white
- Metals: Gold, brass
- Stones: Topaz, citrine
- Herbs: Marjoram, bay leaf

VENUS (Friday)
- Spirits: Amorous, gentle, artistic
- Nature: Loving, creative, harmonious
- Colors: Pink, green, white
- Metals: Silver, copper
- Stones: Emerald, pearl
- Herbs: Mint, lavender

MERCURY (Wednesday)
- Spirits: Swift, cunning, communicative
- Nature: Intelligent, versatile, quick
- Colors: Gray, silver, blue
- Metals: Mercury, tin
- Stones: Quartz, moonstone
- Herbs: Wormwood, pennyroyal

MOON (Monday)
- Spirits: Watery, changeable, emotional
- Nature: Mysterious, intuitive, fluid
- Colors: White, silver, blue
- Metals: Silver, lead
- Stones: Moonstone, pearl
- Herbs: Mugwort, chamomile

Chapter 3: The Names of the Demons

Each demon has multiple names in different languages:
- Hebrew (original)
- Arabic (transmission)
- Latin (Western adaptation)
- Greek (Hellenistic tradition)

Example: The demon Asmodeus
- Hebrew: אשמודאי (Ashmodai)
- Arabic: عشموداي (Ashmuday)
- Latin: Asmodaeus
- Greek: Ασμοδαίος (Asmodaios)

The names must be pronounced correctly, with attention to:
1. Proper vowels and consonants
2. Accents and stress patterns
3. Sacred formulas that accompany the name
4. The specific invocation formula for each demon

WARNING: Incorrect pronunciation can result in:
- Failure of the ritual
- Backlash from the spirit
- Loss of control over the summoning
