CASE FILE: Fournier v. The Accused
Location: Toulouse, France
Date: 1598-1602

TRANSCRIPT OF DEMONOLOGICAL EVIDENCE

EXHIBIT A: THE ACCUSED'S KNOWLEDGE OF DEMON NAMES

The accused was questioned on their knowledge of demonic hierarchies:

Q: Do you know the names of demons?
A: Yes, I studied them in Agrippa's book.

Q: Can you name the seven orders of spirits?
A: Seraphim, Cherubim, Thrones, Dominions, Virtues, Powers, Principalities.

Q: Which sphere does Mercury govern?
A: The sixth sphere, the swift and cunning spirits.

Q: What is the name of the first spirit of Mercury?
A: Vapula, king of the spirits swift and keen.

Q: Can you invoke him?
A: Yes, with the proper formula:
"Vapula, king of spirits swift and keen,
By Mercury's power I thee invoke,
Come to my aid, grant me thy boon,
In name of Father, Son, and Holy Ghost."

EXHIBIT B: THE MATERIALS USED IN SUMMONING

The accused possessed:
1. A book of Agrippa's De Occulta Philosophia (Book III)
2. A circle drawn in chalk on parchment
3. Seven candles corresponding to the seven planets
4. A mirror for Vapula (Mercury's material)
5. Quick-silver (mercury metal) as offering
6. A brass key for Forculus (gatekeeper spirit)
7. Wine and incense as offerings

EXHIBIT C: THE CORRESPONDENCES ESTABLISHED

The accused demonstrated knowledge of:
1. Planetary correspondences (Mercury ↔ Wednesday)
2. Elemental correspondences (Air ↔ Mercury)
3. Temporal correspondences (Wednesday hour for Mercury spirits)
4. Material correspondences (glass, mirrors, quick-silver)
5. Invocation formulas with proper Trinity invocation

EXHIBIT D: THE DEMONIC RESPONSES

The accused reported:
1. Vapula appeared as a small, swift spirit
2. Forculus opened doors and passages
3. Megapenthes brought joy and celebration
4. Pan protected in wild places
5. Panga defended against enemies

EXHIBIT E: THE CHARGES AGAINST THE ACCUSED

The prosecution alleged:
1. Summoning demons for harmful purposes
2. Making pacts with evil spirits
3. Using magical arts to manipulate natural forces
4. Conspiring with other witches
5. Possessing forbidden knowledge without approval

EXHIBIT F: THE DEFENSE ARGUMENTS

The defense argued:
1. The accused studied demonology for scholarly purposes
2. No harmful acts were committed
3. The accused maintained purity of intent
4. The accused sought knowledge, not power
5. The accused invoked the Trinity in all rituals

EXHIBIT G: THE TRIAL VERDICT

The court found:
1. The accused possessed forbidden knowledge
2. The accused summoned demons without ecclesiastical approval
3. The accused used materials for occult purposes
4. The accused demonstrated understanding of correspondences
5. The accused invoked spirits by name and formula

VERDICT: GUILTY OF DEMONOLGY AND OCCULT PRACTICE
PENALTY: DEATH BY HANGING, FOLLOWED BY BURNING OF REMAINS
