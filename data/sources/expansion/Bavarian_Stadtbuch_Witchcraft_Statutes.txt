BAVARIAN STATUTENBUCH (CITY BOOK)
Excerpts on Witchcraft and Demonology
16th Century Bavaria, Holy Roman Empire

STATUTE 45: OF DEMON SUMMONING AND PUNISHMENT

Article I: The Nature of the Crime

It is forbidden for any person, whether male or female, to:
1. Summon demons by name or invocation
2. Make pacts with evil spirits
3. Use magical arts for harmful purposes
4. Conspire with witches or sorcerers
5. Possess knowledge of demonic hierarchies without ecclesiastical approval

Penalty: Death by hanging, followed by burning of remains.

Article II: The Evidence Required

Conviction requires:
1. Confession (if obtained without torture)
2. Witness testimony from at least two credible persons
3. Physical evidence of ritual implements (books, circles, tools)
4. Testimony of the accused's knowledge of demon names and hierarchies
5. Evidence of harmful acts resulting from demonic assistance

Article III: The Role of Demonology in Prosecution

The prosecution must establish:
1. That the accused knew the proper names of demons
2. That the accused understood the correspondences (planetary, elemental, temporal)
3. That the accused performed rituals with proper materials and timing
4. That the accused invoked the Trinity incorrectly or omitted it
5. That the accused sought power rather than knowledge

Article IV: The Defense Against Demonological Charges

The defense may argue:
1. That the accused studied demonology for scholarly purposes only
2. That no harmful acts were committed
3. That the accused never invoked demons by name
4. That the accused maintained purity of intent
5. That the accused sought knowledge, not power

However, mere possession of occult texts is insufficient for conviction.
The prosecution must prove actual summoning or pact-making.

STATUTE 67: OF WITCHES AND THEIR DEMONIC COMPANIONS

Article I: The Nature of Witchcraft

Witchcraft involves:
1. Summoning demons for assistance in harm
2. Making pacts with evil spirits
3. Using magical arts to manipulate natural forces
4. Conspiring with other witches or sorcerers
5. Possessing knowledge of demonic hierarchies without approval

Penalty: Death by burning.

Article II: The Demon as Accomplice

Demons summoned by witches are considered accomplices:
1. They bear responsibility for harmful acts
2. They may be exorcised and sent to hell
3. Their names must be recorded in the trial record
4. Their correspondences must be analyzed for pattern recognition

Article III: The Material Evidence of Witchcraft

Physical evidence includes:
1. Books on demonology (Agrippa, Picatrix, etc.)
2. Ritual implements (circles, wands, candles)
3. Magical materials (herbs, metals, stones)
4. Summoning formulas and invocations
5. Records of demonic correspondences

Article IV: The Ecclesiastical Response

The Church requires:
1. Exorcism of summoned demons
2. Confession of pact-making
3. Renunciation of evil spirits
4. Return of stolen goods or compensation for harm
5. Public penance before the community

STATUTE 89: OF OCCULT TEXTS AND THEIR TRANSMISSION

Article I: The Classification of Occult Literature

Occult texts are divided into:
1. Sacred (approved by Church)
2. Scholarly (studied for knowledge)
3. Forbidden (used for harmful purposes)
4. Heretical (contrary to divine order)

Article II: The Transmission of Knowledge

Knowledge of demonology may be transmitted:
1. Through proper ecclesiastical channels
2. For scholarly study in universities
3. For protective purposes against evil spirits
4. Never for harmful or selfish ends

Article III: The Responsibility of the Scholar

Scholars must:
1. Study texts with pure intent
2. Maintain proper correspondences
3. Avoid summoning without necessity
4. Report harmful practices to authorities
5. Protect the community from demonic influence
