Puritan book, The Pilgrim's Progress,

The spiral map of the journey shows all the

through on his journey from the City of 
Destruction to the Celestial City of 
Jerusalem. At first, he almost sinks in the 
Slough of Despond, in the Valley ofHumilia-

lyon. At VanityFair he is mocked, and later, 
with his companion Hopeful, he meets the 
giant Despair. In the vineyards and gardens 
of Beulah, the Land of Matrimony, the two 
are lovingly cared for before they must 
leave behind their "mortal garments" to 
cross the River of Death, for only in this 
way can they reach their goal, the Golden 
City of Jerusalem.
